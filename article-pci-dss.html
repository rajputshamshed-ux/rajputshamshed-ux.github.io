SECURITY POLICY COMPLIANCE ASSESSMENT & ROADMAP

Mis à jour le 22 Juin 2026 • ⏱️ 5 min de lecture

Executive Summary

RetailMax Corporation is positioning itself for major business growth by pursuing PCI-DSS v4.0, ISO 27001:2022, and SOC 2 Type II certifications. However, the current security policy infrastructure introduces significant compliance and legal risk.

A thorough review reveals that several core policies are completely missing (Incident Response, Data Classification, Access Control), while existing policies are outdated (ranging from 2019 to 2021) and fail to reflect modern security landscapes.

Part A: Gap Analysis (PCI-DSS v4.0 Alignment)

PCI-DSS v4.0 Req Status Gap Description Impact on ISO 27001 / SOC 2
Req 3 & 4: Protect Stored Account Data CRITICAL GAP No Data Classification Policy exists. Cardholder Data (CHD) is not defined, labeled, or restricted. Fail. Violates ISO Annex A.8.1 (Classification) and SOC 2 Confidentiality criteria.
Req 7 & 8: Restrict Access & Auth Users CRITICAL GAP No Access Control Policy. Password Policy (2020) lacks modern NIST/PCI rules (MFA, PAM, 12+ characters). Fail. Violates ISO Annex A.5.15 & A.5.18 and SOC 2 Access Control criteria.
Req 10: Log and Monitor Access GAP Existing Information Security Policy (2019) does not mandate logging architecture or review cycles. Fail. Violates ISO Annex A.8.15 and SOC 2 Security/Monitoring criteria.
Req 12.10: Incident Handling CRITICAL GAP No Incident Response Policy or actionable playbooks exist. Fail. Violates ISO Annex A.5.24 and SOC 2 Availability/Security criteria.
Req 12.1.2: Annual Policy Review GAP All active policies are outdated (3–7 years old) with no evidence of ongoing management review. Fail. Violates ISO Clause 9.2/10.1 (Continuous Improvement).

Part B: Policy Maturity Assessment

[Level 0: Incident Response, Data Class, Access Control] ➔ [Level 1: InfoSec (2019), Password (2020), AUP (2021)]

  • Incident Response Policy: Level 0 (Non-existent)
    Justification: No document or incident response team (IRT) structure exists.
  • Data Classification Policy: Level 0 (Non-existent)
    Justification: No rules define how data is categorized, handled, or securely destroyed.
  • Access Control Policy: Level 0 (Non-existent)
    Justification: Lack of formalized rules regarding the Principle of Least Privilege and user provisioning.

Part D: 12-Month Implementation Roadmap

Phase 1: Foundation & Triage (Months 1–3)

Deliverables: Formal approval and launch of the Incident Response Policy, Access Control Policy, and Data Classification Policy.

Phase 2: Core Modernization & Alignment (Months 4–6)

Deliverables: Complete overhaul of the Information Security Policy and Acceptable Use Policy (AUP).
